About BoardMap NZ
What is this?
BoardMap NZ maps the boards of directors of the NZX top 30 companies and surfaces the connections between them — where the same individual sits on multiple boards, a pattern known as an interlocking directorship. The goal is to provide a clear starting point for understanding how corporate governance in NZ actually looks.
What is an interlocking directorship?
An interlocking directorship occurs when a person sits on the boards of two or more companies simultaneously. In a small economy like New Zealand, this is common and not inherently problematic — experienced directors are in high demand. But the pattern is worth understanding: shared board members can create information flows, alignments of interest, or potential conflicts that are not always visible to outside observers.
Where does the data come from?
All data is sourced from the NZ Companies Register, maintained by the Companies Office (MBIE) and freely available as a public record. Director appointments and resignations are legally required to be filed with the Register. The list of NZX top 30 companies is manually curated based on market capitalisation.
How current is the data?
BoardMap NZ is not a live feed. Data is refreshed manually and reflects the Companies Register at the time of the last update, shown in the top bar.
What the data doesn't show
  • Board observer or advisory roles not formally registered
  • Executive roles (CEO, CFO) — governance roles only
  • Board committee memberships (audit, remuneration, risk)
  • Shareholder or ownership relationships
  • Historical board compositions over time
  • Private or unlisted companies
Methodology
Director names are normalised using fuzzy string matching to resolve inconsistencies across company registrations. Node size reflects data only: company nodes are sized by market cap; director nodes by number of current board seats. Colour encodes sector. No visual choices imply a judgement about any individual or company.
Who built this?
BoardMap NZ is a solo public-interest project. It is free, ad-free, and has no commercial purpose. If you spot an error or have a suggestion, please get in touch.
